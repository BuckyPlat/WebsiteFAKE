The Racing Career of the Nissan GT-R R32

The Birth of a Legend – “Godzilla”

The Nissan Skyline GT-R R32 made its debut in 1989 and quickly earned a fearsome reputation on race tracks around the world. Thanks to its revolutionary ATTESA E-TS all-wheel-drive system and the RB26DETT twin-turbo engine, it dominated almost every motorsport series it entered.

Group A Racing (1989–1993)

The R32 was built to conquer Group A racing — and it did. In Japan’s All Japan Touring Car Championship (JTCC), it won 29 out of 29 races between 1989 and 1993. Its incredible grip and power forced officials to change the rules — a rare honor that showed how far ahead of its time it was.



Australian Touring Car Championship

In Australia, the R32 GT-R’s performance was so overwhelming that the local press dubbed it “Godzilla” — the monster from Japan that crushed everything in its path. It dominated the Australian Touring Car Championship (ATCC), winning multiple titles and the prestigious Bathurst 1000 in 1991 and 1992.



24 Hours of Spa (1991)

The R32 GT-R also took its talents to Europe, entering the 24 Hours of Spa endurance race in Belgium. Despite facing seasoned European manufacturers, it performed brilliantly and proved that Japanese engineering could compete on the world stage in endurance racing.



Legacy and Influence

By the time production ended in 1994, the R32 GT-R had already become a legend. Its technology and performance inspired the R33 and R34 generations and even shaped the design of the modern R35 GT-R. Today, it’s considered a motorsport icon — a car that not only redefined performance but also introduced a new era of Japanese racing dominance.
