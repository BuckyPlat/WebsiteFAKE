Nissan GT-R R32: The Godzilla That Redefined Performance Cars Forever

Japanese legend’s rebirth sparks renewed interest in JDM heritage and motorsport dominance

By Vo Nhan Hong Phuc

Published: 7 October 2025 | 3 min read

In 1989, Nissan introduced a car that would forever change the world of performance — the Skyline GTR R32. With its advanced technology and fierce power, it quickly earned the nickname "Godzilla" from the Australian press for dominating motorsport events and crushing its competition.

Powered by the RB26DETT engine, a 2.6-liter twin-turbo inline-six, and paired with the ATTESA E-TS all-wheel-drive system, the R32 GTR delivered incredible handling and acceleration. It wasn’t just fast — it was a technological masterpiece that pushed the limits of what a road car could do.

The GTR R32’s dominance in Group A racing, particularly in Japan’s Super GT and Australia’s touring car championships, made it a living legend. Even decades later, enthusiasts around the world continue to admire its balance, power, and unmistakable spirit of innovation.
